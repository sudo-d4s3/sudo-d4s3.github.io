Muh_Cyberdeck

2021-01-11 21:54:35

Init

I first learned about cyberdecks through Shadowrun Returns and thought the concept was pretty cool and found this post which has been moved. That post sent me to r/cyberdecks and the first deck I saw was D10D3’s “Cyberdeck v3” which made me want to make one myself.

Now what exactly is a cyberdeck? Well Gibson says “eh fuck it”. (not actually but he did say “… my sense of that a “deck” looked like was extremely low-resolution. Really BIG pixels of visual imagination.")

I’ve been working on the conception for my cyberdeck for about 2 years. There is a lot of concept art for cyberdecks out there but none of them really felt like what I wanted. I finally found one that I really like. I don’t remember where I found it but doing a reverse image search will probably send you to where I got it.

Parts List

Brain

  • ODroid-n2+

Screen

  • unbranded 10.1in screen
  • E-ink display

Keyboard

  • Teensy 3.2
  • Keycaps
  • Big bag ‘o Diodes

Movement

  • Lazy Susan
  • Linear Rail (note: I bought the 200mm version)

The Rail

I collected all the major materials I need yesterday (1/10/21) and got straight to work.

Beforehand I measured everything I could and sketched the three parts

Originally I wanted to be able to slot in and out the keyboard portion of the deck for upgrades later on down the line. I didn’t have enough forsight before I cut the case out of acrylic and didn’t make enough room for the teensy so it has to go in the rail housing making it infeasible to make it modular. (and I forgot to mirror the text on the back)

I printed a 50mm x 50mm x 10mm block to combine the lazy susan and rail together. I wanted to screw everything together but I couldn’t find any screws that fit it. So I committed a cardinal sin and epoxied the rail’s cartridge and the plastic block and screwed the lazy susan to the block. I then had the second most terrifying experiance I’ve ever had when I took a dremel to the screws. (The first was when I used a welding torch, or better put “fire bad”)
