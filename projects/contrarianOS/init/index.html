Intro

2 mins

contrarianOS has been a project I've been wanting to work on for a while. It's a linux distro that does the opposite of whatever the big distros are doing or sometimes just does something because I saw it in another distro and want to try it out.

Some of the ideas I have for contrarianOS are:

  • musl instead of glibc
  • zfs root
  • statically compiled
  • updating means installing a new os in place
  • wayland (ik not really that contrarian bite me)
  • no systemd
  • no pulseaudio
  • no bash
  • linuxlibre kernel (This is still somehow contrarian, I love it when hardware companies make their firmware and drivers proprietary even though they don't make money off of it)
  • realtime kernel patch (because that sounds funny)
  • compile everything for x86_64-v3

Somethings I haven't made up my mind on:

  • aio tool set (busybox or toybox)
    • or ubase/sbase

Ideas I'm not sure are possible:

  • changing the rootfs structure

Interesting distros I'm basing these ideas on:

  • NixOS
  • Guix
  • GobboLinux
  • Oasis
  • Alpine
  • Void
